## Who this is for

Standalone dialysis centers and chains, hospital daycare units run as profit centres, chemotherapy daycares, and endoscopy/cath daycares. Your unit economics live or die on chair utilisation, consumable cost per session, and scheme reimbursement cycles.

---

## The problems we solve at this scale

**Slot chaos.** Three shifts, fixed patients, machine maintenance windows, HBsAg/HCV isolation machines, and emergency add-ons — on a whiteboard. {Product} schedules by machine and shift, enforces isolation-machine rules automatically, and reschedules a maintenance day in one drag.

**The scheme reimbursement treadmill.** PMJAY and state-scheme dialysis runs on per-session packages with documentation requirements. {Product} generates the session evidence packet automatically and tracks every claim from session to settlement, so rejections fall and follow-up stops depending on one clerk's memory.

**Consumables eating margin.** Dialysers (with reuse counts where permitted), bloodlines, heparin, EPO — tracked per session, per patient. You see cost per session against reimbursement per session, per scheme, in the MIS.

**The flowsheet burden.** Pre-weight, vitals every 30 minutes, machine parameters, post-weight — nurses chart at the chair on a tablet, and the session record builds itself. Recurring patients carry their prescription (dry weight, dialyser, duration, anticoagulation) forward automatically.

{Product}'s [Daycare Workflows module](/product/daycare-workflows) treats the session — not the admission — as the unit of work. ## Frequently asked questions

**Q: We run dialyser reuse. Does the system track reuse counts safely?**
A: Yes — per-patient dialyser identity, reuse count against your protocol's limit, and automatic blocking at the limit, with the reprocessing log attached to each session.

**Q: Our patients are fixed-slot regulars. Does scheduling handle standing bookings?**
A: Standing slots (Mon-Wed-Fri 1st shift, machine 4) are the default pattern; the schedule auto-populates monthly and flags conflicts when a machine goes down.

**Q: Can a hospital run its daycare on {Product} while using another HIMS?**
A: Yes — the daycare module runs standalone with its own billing, or integrated if the hospital later moves to {Product} fully.
