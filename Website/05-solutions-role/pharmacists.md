## A day with {Product}, in the pharmacy

**Counter.** Prescriptions arrive electronically the moment the doctor signs — no deciphering handwriting, no calling the ward to confirm a dose. The system suggests batch (FEFO — first expiry, first out), checks the allergy banner one last time, and bills in the same motion. Substitution within the hospital formulary is logged with the pharmacist's identity.

**Wards.** Indents from nursing stations land in your queue, ward-wise. Issue against indent, and ward stock and patient billing update together — the end of 'consumed but never billed'.

**Back office.** Reorder levels calculate from real consumption, not guesswork; purchase orders generate in one review. GRN against PO with invoice matching. Expiry report every Monday: what to return, what to push, what to write off — before it's money in the bin.

**Registers.** Schedule H1, narcotics (with double-lock witness entries), and TPA-audit stock statements print in prescribed formats from data you already entered once.

## What changes for you

**Dispensing errors drop.** Electronic orders end transcription mistakes; the final allergy/interaction screen at the counter is the last net before the patient.

**Dead stock and expiry losses shrink.** FEFO picking, expiry horizons, and slow-moving reports typically recover several percent of pharmacy revenue.

**Audits stop being archaeology.** Every strip traces from purchase to patient. Schedule H1 inspections take minutes.

**The purchase cycle runs on data.** Consumption-based reorder, supplier rate comparison, and bounce tracking — negotiation power you can print.

## What pharmacists ask us

**Q: We sell OTC retail to walk-ins too. Same system?**
A: Yes — retail counter sales with GST invoicing run alongside prescription dispensing, with separate analytics for each.

**Q: How are narcotics and psychotropics handled?**
A: Dual-custody issue entries, witness sign-off, running balance registers in statutory format, and discrepancy alerts the moment a count doesn't tally.

**Q: Our supplier invoices never match POs. Does GRN handle partials and rate differences?**
A: Yes — partial receipts, rate variance flags with approval routing, free goods, and scheme quantities are all standard GRN cases.
