# Messaging & voice guide

## Positioning
{Product} is the EHR built for how India actually practices medicine.

## Four proof pillars (every page maps to at least one)
1. Specialty-deep, not specialty-listed — show the partograph, the dialysis flowsheet, the TNM staging form.
2. India-native by architecture — ABDM/ABHA, 9 Indian languages in the clinical UI, lakh/crore billing, GST, TPA, NABH audit trails, DPDP compliance.
3. Works on the worst machine in the hospital — 1366×768 screens, flaky bandwidth, shared nursing PCs, offline tolerance.
4. Clinically safe by design — alert severity levels, allergy-first patient banner, color-never-alone accessibility.

## Voice
- Confident, clinical, plain. Write like a senior doctor explaining to a hospital owner.
- Sentence case everywhere, including headings and buttons.
- No "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "world-class", "seamless". Replace hype with specifics: "OPD billing in under 40 seconds" beats "lightning-fast billing".
- Numbers use Indian formats: ₹1,20,000 · 10 Jun 2026 · 24h time in clinical contexts.
- Every claim gets a proof point (feature, standard, or metric). If we can't prove it, we don't say it.

## Audience lenses
- Promoter/CEO: revenue, NABH, patient experience, control. Pages: Home, Pricing, Case studies.
- CMO/Doctors: speed of documentation, specialty depth, safety. Pages: Specialty, Product modules.
- IT head: security, interoperability, uptime, DPDP. Pages: Platform hub.
- Single-specialty chains: multi-center control, standardized protocols. Pages: Facility type.

Primary: "Book a demo" · Secondary: "See it in your specialty" · Tertiary: "Talk to us on WhatsApp"
