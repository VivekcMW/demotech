## The problems we solve at this scale

**The multi-unit blind spot.** Each unit runs its own instance, its own item masters, its own tariffs — so network-level questions take a week of Excel. {Product} runs multi-entity natively: shared masters with local overrides, centralised tariff governance, consolidated MIS, and unit-level autonomy where it belongs.

**Quaternary documentation depth.** Transplant registries, tumour boards, CTVS perfusion records, bone-marrow transplant protocols — generic EMRs collapse here. Our specialty modules go this deep because they were designed from these workflows, not retrofitted.

**Integration estates.** ERP, payroll, existing PACS, national registries, insurance gateways. {Product} exposes FHIR R4 APIs and standard HL7/DICOM interfaces, and we publish the API documentation openly — your integration partners don't need our permission to read it.

**Governance and DPDP.** Consent management, role-based access with break-glass auditing, data residency in India, and per-entity data segregation are architecture, not add-ons.
