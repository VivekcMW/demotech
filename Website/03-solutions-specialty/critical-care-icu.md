## The problems we built around
- **Paper flowsheets at hourly frequency:** ICU nursing drowns in transcription; trends hide in grids of handwriting.
- **Device data is read aloud, not recorded:** Monitors, ventilators, and pumps know everything; the chart knows what someone copied.
- **Severity and audit scores are weekend projects:** APACHE/SOFA and NABH ICU indicators get computed retrospectively, if at all.

## How ICU runs on AarogyaEHR
### Electronic flowsheets
Hourly vitals, ventilation parameters, infusion rates, and intake-output on one scrollable grid; stale-data indicators per unit policy (5-minute ICU threshold).
### Device integration
Monitor and ventilator data streams in automatically where interfaces exist; manual-entry fallback is two taps on a tablet.
### Severity scoring
APACHE II / SOFA / qSOFA computed from charted data; daily trend visible on the unit dashboard.
### ICU dashboard (dark theme default)
Per-bed tiles with large vital values, threshold coloring, and critical pulse animation that stops after two cycles — alarm fatigue is a design constraint here.
### Care bundles & checklists
VAP/CLABSI/CAUTI bundle checklists on rounds, sedation holidays, DVT prophylaxis prompts; compliance feeds NABH indicators.
### Handover & family communication
Shift handover summaries auto-drafted from the flowsheet; structured family-counseling notes with language-tagged records.

## Frequently asked questions
**Is dark theme mandatory in ICU?**
Theme is per-workstation; ICU deployments default to dark for night shifts and can switch instantly.

**Which devices integrate?**
Common monitor/ventilator families via standard protocols; we validate your inventory during onboarding.

**Can intensivists round remotely?**
The unit dashboard and flowsheets are accessible for tele-ICU review with full audit trails.
