# Your data, in standard formats, through open doors

A hospital system that traps data is a future migration crisis. {Product} is built on health-data standards and exposes them — so your data works with the ecosystem, and leaves with you if you ever go.

---

## The standards stack

**FHIR R4.** The primary API surface. Patients, encounters, observations, medications, diagnostic reports, and documents are exposed as FHIR resources, including the ABDM FHIR profiles India's exchange runs on. Your integration partners read our published API docs without signing anything.

**HL7v2.** The workhorse for device and legacy interfaces — ADT, ORM/ORU for lab and radiology orders and results. Decades of installed equipment speak it; so do we.

**DICOM.** Modality worklist, store, query/retrieve for imaging. CT, MRI, CR/DR, ultrasound connect to {Product} PACS directly; existing third-party PACS coexist via standard DICOM exchange.

**Terminologies.** LOINC for lab observations, SNOMED CT for clinical terms, ICD-10 for diagnoses, with mapping maintained in the product — coded data is what makes records computable, claims cleaner, and analytics honest.

**ASTM/POCT** for lab analysers and point-of-care devices.

---

## What connects in practice

- Lab analysers (two-way) and imaging modalities (worklist + store)
- Existing PACS, standalone LIS/RIS during transition periods
- ERP/accounting (SAP, Oracle, Tally) via finance posting interfaces
- National programmes and registries where APIs exist (ABDM consent-mediated exchange first among them)
- Insurance/TPA ecosystems as their digital rails mature

---

## The exit-rights clause, in public

Your data is yours. Full structured export — patient master, encounters, results, documents, finance — in standard formats (FHIR bundles, CSV, DICOM), free of charge, at any time, including if you leave. We put this on the website because vendors who won't say it are telling you something.
