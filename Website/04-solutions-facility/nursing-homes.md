## Who this is for

Family-run and proprietor-led nursing homes and small hospitals: 10–50 beds, typically 2–10 doctors (often owner-clinicians), one OT, basic lab, in-house pharmacy. The owner is often the senior surgeon, the administrator, and the final approver of every refund — all at once.

---

## The problems we solve at this scale

**Everything depends on one or two people.** The owner signs off discharges at 11 pm because billing can't be trusted to run itself. {Product} makes discharge billing automatic and auditable — every charge traces to an order, a ward entry, or a pharmacy issue — so the owner approves by exception, not line by line.

**Revenue leakage you can't see.** Unbilled consumables, missed investigation charges, and "adjusted" bills quietly cost small hospitals 5–12% of IPD revenue. Charge-capture at the point of care closes the gap.

**Compliance registers eaten by paper.** MTP register, birth/death registers, OT register, biomedical waste — inspections mean panic. {Product} maintains statutory registers digitally and prints them in the prescribed formats.

**Staff churn.** Nurses and front-desk staff change often. A system they learn in a day — with screens in their language — is the difference between adoption and abandonment.

## How {Product} fits your facility

{Product} for nursing homes is deliberately simple on the surface: a bed board the whole hospital understands at a glance, one-screen IPD case sheets, and billing that follows the patient automatically from admission to discharge. Deposits, interim bills, and final settlement are standard workflow, not customisation.

The OT register, anaesthesia record, and consent forms (in the patient's language) are built in. Pharmacy is linked to prescriptions, so dispensing and billing happen in one motion. Daily MIS — collections, occupancy, OT count, expiring stock — reaches the owner's phone every evening.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Our nurses are not comfortable in English. Will they use it?**
A: Nursing screens — vitals, medication administration, intake/output — run in 9 Indian languages. Drug names and doses stay in standard form for safety; everything else is in the language your staff reads fastest.

**Q: We do MLC and police cases. Is that covered?**
A: Yes — MLC flagging, injury documentation, and the casualty register are standard in the Emergency module, with formats matching what local authorities expect.

**Q: Can we start with billing only and add clinical later?**
A: Yes, and most nursing homes do. Billing + bed board first, pharmacy second, clinical documentation third. Each step works without forcing the next.
