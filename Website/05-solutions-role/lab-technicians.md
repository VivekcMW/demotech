## A day with {Product}, in the lab

**Collection.** Orders generate barcoded labels with tube-type and volume guidance. Scan at collection — time-stamped chain of custody starts. Home-collection samples arrive with the same barcodes already on them.

**Accession & run.** Scan-in routes each sample to its department worklist. Analysers receive the order list automatically (two-way interface), run, and return results — no keyboard between the machine and the report. Manual tests (peripheral smears, micro) get structured entry screens with your lab's reference ranges.

**Validate.** Auto-validation clears normals per your rules; deltas against the patient's previous results, criticals, and flags route to the pathologist's queue. Critical values trigger the documented-notification workflow — who was called, when, read-back confirmed.

**QC.** Daily controls chart onto Levey-Jennings automatically; Westgard violations block the run until acknowledged. EQAS submissions and calibration logs live where the NABL assessor expects them.

## What changes for you

**Retyping ends.** The interface library covers the common Indian installed base (chemistry, haematology, immunoassay, coagulation, electrolytes), and new instruments are added during implementation.

**TAT becomes visible and fixable.** Per-stage timestamps (collected → received → run → validated → released) show exactly where delays live — collection rounds, machine queues, or validation.

**Repeats and rejects get tracked, not absorbed.** Haemolysed, clotted, QNS — rejection reasons log at receipt with automatic recollection requests, and the monthly rejection report drives pre-analytical fixes.

**NABL stops being seasonal.** The evidence trail accumulates daily as a by-product of working.

## What lab teams ask us

**Q: Our analysers are old. Will they interface?**
A: Most instruments with serial or network output interface fine — ASTM and HL7 cover the majority. Where a legacy machine truly can't, a rapid-entry screen with double-entry verification minimises risk.

**Q: Who controls auto-validation rules?**
A: Your pathologist — rules per test, per flag, per delta threshold, fully audit-trailed and adjustable anytime.

**Q: Can we handle outsourced (referred-out) tests?**
A: Yes — referral lab management with sample manifests, pending-from-referral tracking, and their reports attached into the patient's record on return.
