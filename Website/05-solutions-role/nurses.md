## A day with {Product}, on the ward

**Shift start.** Handover isn't a tattered notebook — it's an SBAR summary per patient, auto-built from the last shift's charting, with pending tasks carried forward. You know your six patients in five minutes.

**Medication round.** The e-MAR shows what's due, by bed. Scan the patient band, scan the drug — the five rights check themselves. Look-alike/sound-alike pairs are visually separated; high-alert drugs ask for a second nurse's verification. Given, held, refused — one tap each, with reasons.

**Through the shift.** Vitals on the tablet at the bed; early-warning scores calculate themselves and quietly escalate when a patient trends wrong. Intake/output, wound care, and care-plan tasks tick off as you go. Doctor's new orders appear on your worklist instantly — no chasing the file.

**Shift end.** Handover is already written, because you charted as you worked. You leave on time.

## What changes for you

**Charting time drops by hours per shift.** Tablet-at-the-bedside replaces transcribing from scraps of paper at the nursing station at 9 pm.

**Medication safety stops depending on memory.** The 5-rights check, allergy banner, and dose-range flags run on every administration — protection for the patient and for you.

**Nothing falls between shifts.** Tasks, pending reports, and "watch this patient" notes carry forward in the system, not in goodwill.

**You work in your language.** Nursing screens run in 9 Indian languages; drug names and doses stay standard for safety. The 12-hour-shift night mode (dark theme) was designed for ICU lighting.

## What nurses ask us

**Q: We're short-staffed. Doesn't a system add work?**
A: It removes the double work — charting on paper and then copying registers. One entry feeds the chart, the handover, the registers, and the NABH indicators.

**Q: What if the tablet or network fails mid-round?**
A: Charting continues offline and syncs when the network returns. The ward also keeps desktop access as a fallback.

**Q: Senior sisters approve our charting. Is there a review flow?**
A: Yes — shift-incharge review queues, countersign where your policy requires it, and clear audit trails that protect the nurse who charted correctly.
