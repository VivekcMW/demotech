## A day with {Product}, as an administrator

**7:30 am.** The daily digest on your phone: yesterday's collections by mode and counter, current census by ward, today's OT list, pending discharges, TPA claims ageing past 30 days, and stock items below reorder. Five minutes, full picture.

**Through the day.** A discount beyond policy routes to you for approval with the full bill context — one tap, logged. The NABH coordinator needs Q2 medication-error and fall rates; they're on the quality dashboard already.

## What changes for you

**Revenue integrity becomes systematic.** Every chargeable event — order, consumable, bed-day, procedure — posts automatically. Unbilled-services and bill-modification audit reports surface leakage and 'adjustments' that used to be invisible.

**Discount and refund governance gets teeth.** Authority limits by role, approval routing, and a complete trail — generosity becomes a policy, not a habit.

**Decisions get data.** Occupancy, ALOS, OT utilisation, consultant productivity, payer mix, and department P&L are live queries, not annual exercises.

**Compliance becomes continuous.** NABH indicators, statutory registers, and audit trails accumulate from daily operations.

## What administrators ask us

**Q: Our consultants are on revenue share. Does the system compute it?**
A: Yes — per-consultant share rules (percentage, slab, or fixed, by service category), automatic monthly statements, and deduction handling, ending the spreadsheet disputes.

**Q: Can I see which user modified a bill?**
A: Every modification — who, when, what changed, from what to what — is in the audit trail, with a standing report of post-finalisation changes.

**Q: We run on Tally for accounts. Does this replace it?**
A: No — {Product} posts summarised vouchers to Tally (and other accounting systems), keeping your CA's workflow intact while the hospital side becomes airtight.
