## A day with {Product}, at the TPA desk

**Admission.** A cashless patient arrives: eligibility details, payer-specific tariff, and the pre-auth form pre-filled from the clinical record. You attach the requirement checklist that this specific TPA actually asks for — the system remembers each payer's quirks.

**Mid-stay.** Enhancement requests fire when the running bill approaches the approved limit, with the interim clinical summary attached. Query letters from the TPA land in the case timeline with response deadlines tracked.

**Discharge.** The claim packet — final bill in the payer's format, discharge summary, investigation reports, implant stickers where applicable — assembles from the record. Submission logged, acknowledgement filed.

**Follow-up.** The ageing worklist sorts claims by days outstanding and value. Deductions get categorised (tariff difference, non-payable, documentation) so patterns become negotiations, not losses. Settlement posts against the claim, and the patient file finally closes.

## What changes for you

**Rejections fall.** Most rejections are documentation and tariff errors; pre-filled forms, payer-specific checklists, and correct-first-time pricing remove the causes.

**Deduction patterns become evidence.** Categorised deduction analytics per payer arm your renewal negotiations.

**The desk survives staff changes.** Every case's full history lives in the system, not in one executive's drawer and memory.
