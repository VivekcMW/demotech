## The problems we solve at this scale

**Departments that don't talk.** Lab on one vendor, billing on another, EMR abandoned. Every interface is a fight. {Product} replaces the patchwork — orders, results, images, charges, and notes are one data model, not five integrations.

**The TPA cash-flow choke.** Cashless approvals, query letters, deductions, and 60-day receivables strangle mid-size hospitals. {Product}'s TPA desk tracks every case from pre-auth to settlement, flags ageing claims, and assembles the document packet (discharge summary, bills, reports) automatically.

**Clinical adoption failure.** Consultants reject generic EMRs. {Product}'s specialty depth — the actual cardiology, ortho, OBG, nephro workflows — is why doctors at this tier use it instead of dictating around it.

**NABH evidence collection.** Accreditation audits consume months of manual register-building. Quality indicators, audit trails, and committee reports generate from live data.

## How {Product} fits your facility

This is {Product}'s home ground. The full clinical loop runs digitally: OPD consult → investigations ordered → LIS/PACS results back to the chart with abnormal flags → admission → e-MAR on the wards → OT documentation → discharge summary assembled from the record, not retyped → coded bill → TPA claim.

Role-based shells mean the front desk, nurse, consultant, and TPA executive each see their own working screen — nobody navigates a monolith. The ICU dashboard runs in dark theme for night shifts. The medical director's MIS shows occupancy, specialty-wise revenue, infection rates, and clinical quality indicators without asking anyone to compile them.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: We have 8 years of data in our current system. Can you migrate it?**
A: Patient master, balances, and stock migrate fully. Historical clinical records migrate as searchable documents attached to the patient, with structured migration for lab history where the old system can export it.

**Q: Our consultants are visiting, not full-time. Will they document?**
A: Visiting consultants get a phone/tablet view for rounds — see results, write progress notes by voice or template, sign discharge summaries remotely. Less friction than finding a ward computer.

**Q: How does this help with NABH specifically?**
A: Audit trails, time-stamped records, medication administration records, quality indicator dashboards (medication errors, falls, HAI rates), and committee-ready reports are generated from operational data — evidence accumulates as a by-product of working.
