## The problems we built around
- **PAC happens, proof doesn't:** Fitness given on a slip; the assessment behind it is unrecoverable at audit.
- **Intra-op records are reconstructed post-op:** The anaesthesia chart gets filled at the end, from memory, during turnover pressure.
- **Pain service rounds run on pocket notebooks:** Epidural and PCA follow-ups have no structured trail.

## How anaesthesia runs on AarogyaEHR
### Pre-anaesthesia checkup
Structured PAC with ASA grade, airway scores, comorbidity optimization plans; fitness status gates the OT schedule automatically.
### Intra-op charting
Time-axis anaesthesia chart on an OT tablet: agents, fluids, events, and vitals; templated technique notes (GA/spinal/epidural/blocks).
### Recovery & discharge scoring
Aldrete/PADSS scoring in recovery with criteria-based shift-out; handoff notes to the ward auto-generated.
### Acute pain service
Epidural/PCA follow-up rounds with structured assessment, dose adjustments, and complication flags.
### Difficult airway registry
Flag once, warned forever — the airway alert travels on the patient banner to every future episode.

## Frequently asked questions
**Does the difficult-airway flag persist?**
Yes — it joins the allergy-class banner alerts that never collapse or hide.

**Can pediatric dosing be enforced?**
Weight-based ceilings apply to anaesthetic drugs exactly as in Pediatrics.

**Is the chart medico-legally timestamped?**
Every entry is server-timestamped with editor identity; late entries are marked as such.
