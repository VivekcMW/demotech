# Case studies

## The standard (why ours read differently)

Every case study names the customer, states baseline numbers, reports results with timeframes, and includes a 'what was hard' section — because case studies without friction are advertising, and buyers know it.

## Template

**Header:** Facility name, city, type, beds, specialties, go-live date
**The before:** the operational reality in numbers (collections cycle, discharge TAT, documentation state)
**Why they moved:** the triggering event, in the customer's words
**The rollout:** phases, duration, what was hard, what we'd do differently
**The after:** measured results with timeframes
**Quote:** the administrator or owner, attributed
**Stack:** modules in use, integrations live

## Launch case-study slate (to be produced with founding customers)

1. **A 120-bed multi-specialty hospital** — TPA receivables and discharge TAT story
2. **A 30-bed nursing home** — owner-dependence to system-dependence; revenue-leakage recovery
3. **A dialysis chain (6 centers)** — scheme billing, machine utilisation, standing schedules
4. **An eye-care chain** — camp-to-surgery conversion funnel and counsellor analytics
5. **A diagnostic lab (B2B-heavy)** — analyser interfacing, TAT, and client-ledger peace
6. **A clinic group (8 doctors)** — the 90-second consultation measured before/after
7. **An ICU/critical-care unit** — night-shift charting, early-warning escalations, dark theme in use

## Proof formats

Written study + 3-minute video walkthrough filmed in the facility + one-page PDF for sales follow-up. Metrics independently verifiable from system reports shown on camera.
