## The problems we built around
- **ACS minutes disappear into forms:** Thrombolysis and PCI decisions need a record that keeps pace with the crash cart.
- **Cath lab data lives in silos:** Procedure notes, hardware used, and billing rarely reconcile — stents go unbilled or undocumented.
- **Follow-up is where cardiology outcomes live:** Post-PCI and heart-failure patients need structured recall, not memory.

## How cardiology runs on AarogyaEHR
### ACS pathway mode
Chest-pain triage with timestamped milestones (ECG, troponin, decision, wire-crossing), pre-built STEMI order sets, door-to-needle/balloon analytics.
### Cath lab documentation
Structured procedure notes with vessel diagrams, hardware capture by barcode (stents/balloons/wires) flowing to billing and the implant registry.
### Echo & ECG reporting
Template-driven echo reports with measurement tables, comparison with priors, ECG attachment and interpretation library.
### Device & implant registry
Pacemaker/ICD registry with implant details, programming notes, and replacement-due recalls.
### Heart failure & post-PCI clinics
Protocolized follow-up templates, GDMT titration tracking, DAPT duration alerts.
